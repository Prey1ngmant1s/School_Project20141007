Mutualism

Mutualism is any relationship between individuals of different species where both individuals benefit. In general, only lifelong interactions involving close physical and biochemical contact can properly be considered symbiotic . Mutualistic relationships may be either obligate for both species, obligate for one but facultative for the other, or facultative for both. Many biologists restrict the definition of symbiosis to close mutualist relationships. Symbiosis of this kind come from many animals and other forms of life, Insects have many Mutalistic relatsion with plants but there is no better example than the bee. While the bee gathers nectar it also takes pollen from plants and spreads it allowing the flowers to thrive and the bee to contnue to nest and eat.
